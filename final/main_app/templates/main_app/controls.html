Cybersecurity Strategy

1-1 Cybersecurity Strategy

To ensure that cybersecurity plans, goals, initiatives and projects are contributing to compliance with related laws and regulations

1-1-1

A cybersecurity plan must be created, approved, and supported by the organization's leader or their representative. It should comply with the law.

1-1-2

A roadmap must be executed to implement the cybersecurity strategy.

1-1-3

The cybersecurity strategy must be reviewed periodically according to planned intervals or upon changes to related laws and regulations.

1-2 Cybersecurity Management

To ensure Authorizing Officialâ€™s support in implementing and managing cybersecurity programs within the organization as per related laws and regulations.

1-2-1

A dedicated cybersecurity function (e.g., division, department) must be established within the organization. This function must be independent from the Information Technology/Information Communication and Technology (IT/ICT) functions. It is highly recommended that this cybersecurity function reports directly to the head of the organization or his/her delegate while ensuring that this does not result in a conflict of interest. 1

1-2-2

The position of cybersecurity function head (e.g., CISO), and related supervisory and critical positions within the function, must be filled with full-time and experienced Saudi cybersecurity professionals.

1-2-3

A cybersecurity steering committee must be established by the Authorizing Official to ensure the support and implementation of the cybersecurity programs and initiatives within the organization. Committee members, roles and responsibilities, and governance framework must be defined, documented and approved. The committee must include the head of the cybersecurity function as one of its members. It is highly recommended that the committee reports directly to the head of the organization or his/her delegate while ensuring that this does not result in a conflict of interest.

1-Creation of the Plan: Develop a comprehensive cybersecurity plan that addresses various aspects of security, such as risk assessment, threat management, incident response, data protection, and employee training.
2-Approval Process: Once the plan is drafted, it should be reviewed by relevant stakeholders, including top management and legal advisors, to ensure its effectiveness and compliance with organizational objectives and legal requirements.
3-Support from Leadership: The plan should be endorsed and supported by the organization's leader or their designated representative, such as a Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) or Chief Technology Officer (CTO). Their backing is crucial for allocating resources and prioritizing cybersecurity initiatives.
4-Compliance with Laws and Regulations: Ensure that the cybersecurity plan aligns with applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards relevant to the organization's operations. This may include data protection laws, industry-specific regulations, and international standards like ISO 27001.
5-Regular Review and Updates: The cybersecurity plan should be regularly reviewed and updated to adapt to evolving threats, changes in technology, and modifications in legal requirements. This ensures its ongoing relevance and effectiveness in mitigating cybersecurity risks.
6-Training and Awareness: Provide training and awareness programs to employees to ensure their understanding of the cybersecurity plan and their role in maintaining security within the organization.

1. Define Goals:Clearly outline the objectives and goals of the cybersecurity strategy.
2. Assess Current State: Evaluate the existing cybersecurity infrastructure, policies, and practices.
3. Identify Risks: Identify potential cybersecurity risks and vulnerabilities within the organization.
4. Develop Action Plan: Create a detailed action plan that outlines specific tasks, responsibilities, and timelines for implementing the cybersecurity strategy.
5. Allocate Resources: Allocate necessary resources, including budget, personnel, and technology, to support the implementation process.
6. Implement Controls: Implement security controls and measures to mitigate identified risks and enhance cybersecurity posture.
7. Monitor Progress: Continuously monitor the progress of the implementation process and adjust the plan as needed.
8. Training and Awareness: Provide training and awareness programs to educate employees about cybersecurity best practices and their role in maintaining security.
9. Testing and Evaluation: Regularly test and evaluate the effectiveness of implemented security measures to ensure they are achieving the desired outcomes.
10. Continuous Improvement: Foster a culture of continuous improvement by learning from incidents, updating policies and procedures, and staying informed about emerging threats and technologies.
